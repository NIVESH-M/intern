Understanding Procurement

Procurement is the process of acquiring goods, services, or works from third-party vendors through methods like direct purchase, competitive bidding, or tendering. It involves selecting and acquiring the right quality and quantity while ensuring timely delivery.

Managing procurement can consume a significant portion of a company's resources, and it often operates within defined budgets. The effectiveness of procurement can directly impact a company's profitability and operations.

Procurement encompasses various functions, making it a critical part of a company's strategy. For instance, environmentally conscious companies focus on engaging green suppliers, while others align procurement with specific goals.

Procurement processes involve purchase planning, supplier selection, price negotiation, and inventory control. It requires collaboration from different areas of a company for success.

Procurement Life Cycle

Organizations typically follow a procurement life cycle, including these steps:

  1. Identifying needs and analyzing requirements, followed by defining technical specifications.
  2. Conducting external market analysis to assess competitiveness and industry trends.
  3. Performing cost analysis to make informed make-buy decisions.
  4. Identifying suitable suppliers through various means, such as trade shows.
  5. Ensuring confidentiality through non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) before sharing sensitive information.
  6. Engaging in competitive bidding, considering factors like quality, price, and delivery.
  7. Negotiating and formalizing contracts that outline terms and details.
  8. Managing logistics and supplier performance based on contract terms.
  9. Reviewing experiences and considering supplier changes when necessary.
  10. Establishing supplier relationships with formal governance for strategic goods or services.
